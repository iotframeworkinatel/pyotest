Detection Stability Across Seeds

Do ML-guided tests achieve consistent vulnerability detection across random simulation seeds?
Experiment
Phase 1 (AutoML Framework), realistic simulation mode, 3 independent seeds (42, 123, 777). Each seed is a separate experimental replication — no cross-contamination.
Why It Matters
If detection rates vary wildly by seed, results are not robust. Low inter-seed variance confirms the ML approach generalizes beyond specific random environmental conditions.
Finding
Inter-seed standard deviation σ=0.002 — negligible variance across all 3 seeds × 5 frameworks (210K tests) confirms stable detection.

Unexpected Result
ML does not outperform baselines on raw detection rate.

In realistic mode, all strategies converge to ~49–50%. Environment randomness (service outages, patches, cred rotations) dominates any ML signal.

In deterministic mode, random sampling slightly beats ML (p=0.003), likely due to uniform protocol coverage vs ML's skewed selection.
Unexpected Finding
Note: Raw detection rate may not be the right metric — AUC and calibration quality may better capture ML's advantages in test prioritization.
